Typographic Hierarchy

Use CSS to style a long-form text.

 

Step One

Analyze the text and code for The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction Identify the semantic tags that designate the typographic hierarchy.

Step Two

Import the text into InDesign and use Paragraph and Character styles to create three type hierarchy sketches using only fonts available at Google Fonts. Use as many 8.5x11 pages as you need, and print and bring them to class.

Step Three

Choose and refine a single type hierarchy in InDesign.

Step Four

Using CSS styles, translate your InDesign hierarchy to the web. Investigate how you can replicate these styles on the web, and test to see if they are as effective online as in print. How does scrolling change the need for line spacing or rules? What type sizes are most effective?

Step Five

Refine your print and digital type hierarchies to create a style suite while accounting for usability differences.

Step Six

Add navigation to your hierarchy. Experiment with additional styling.
