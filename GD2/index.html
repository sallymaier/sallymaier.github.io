Projects

This course provides extended study of graphic design principles and their application to more complex and comprehensive solutions. Experimentation, research, conceptual thinking, and process are emphasized in design for the screen. Students learn essential design tools and techniques for the development of interactive media. Students work with html and css to understand code as a fundamental building block for their design compositions. Prerequisite: GD 200 (Graphic Design 1).

Learning Outcomes

Articulate design fundamentals and apply them to digital media.

Demonstrate an understanding of hierarchy as it applies to interactive media.

Employ concepts, processes, and production tools for interaction design.

Develop a process that adapts to new tools, best practices and mediums.

Develop an understanding of interaction as an essential component of communication.

Class Meetings

Classes will consist of critiques, presentations, demonstrations, work sessions, and discussion. We will meet as a large group, in small groups, and in individual meetings at different points in the semester. Generally class will be treated as structured work time where feedback and help are readily available. You should always be prepared to work on the current project (or short in class assignments) whenever we meet, as well as to show progress each session.

Attendance

Students are expected to attend all meetings of each class in which they are enrolled. Unexcused absences from as few as three classes in a three-credit course will result in a failing grade.

An unexcused absence on a critique day signifigantly lower your grade for that project.

Excused absences are the following and must be documented: personal illness, court appearance, death in the family; email as soon as you know you will be missing class. You are responsible for any material missed, do not expect me to recap. If you miss a critique day, your work is due at the next earliest possible date. Use your good judgment and get me your materials as soon as is possible. If they aren’t turned in by the following week’s class meeting, you’ll begin to lose a letter grade per week late.

Work and Critiques

If you are having difficulty understanding an assignment or completing your work, contact me right away.

Be prepared for class every day that we meet. Please bring all the necessary tools, files, and sketches to work in class. You must meet all project deadlines. Technical problems are not an excuse. Late work will automatically lower your grade.

If you have not finished work for a critique, come to class anyway to at least engage the discussion. Your assignment, however, will still be considered late.

Work on assignments for this studio only during our class sessions; no personal email, social networking, phone calls, texting or chat during critiques, demos or lectures. Trust me, I see you, and this will affect your grade.

Projects

We will be completing 3 main projects this semester. Through them we will explore modular and systems design across multiple mediums and interaction design. The final design solutions will vary in medium and scope. These projects are designed to further your understanding and experience of design fundamentals and practice, while focusing on the rapid evolution of technology that continues to change how we communicate visually. I will post project briefs as the semester progresses clarifying project goals, deliverables and constraints. Most projects will include pre-made code to help you that you will be required to use. Save sketches and draft versions to show a progression of your work.
